<Subtitle title={"Psychological biases in Life (and in Agile development too):"} icon="twemoji:brain"/>

### <h3 class="hidden">Confirmation bias</h3>
<Card title="Confirmation bias" icon="emoji:ballot-box-with-ballot">
  Favoring information that confirms our **existing beliefs**, leading to blind spots
  <p>`Seeing what you want to see` <small>might lead to missed opportunities and rejections</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Hick's law</h3>
<Card title="Hick's law" icon="emoji:thinking-face">
  The more choices that are available to the user, the longer it will take them to reach a decision
  <p>`Challenge to make the right choice of many`<small>can lead to procrastination</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Loss aversion</h3>
<Card title="Loss aversion" icon="emoji:chart-decreasing">
  Disliking **losses** more than enjoying gains
  <p>`Hating to lose` <small>might lead to missed opportunities and risk-averse behavior</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Planning fallacy</h3>
<Card title="Planning fallacy" icon="emoji:spiral-calendar">
  Underestimating required the **time and effort**
  <p>`False belief it's just copy-paste`<small>might lead to missed deadlines and overtimes</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Decision fatigue</h3>
<Card title="Decision fatigue" icon="emoji:anxious-face-with-sweat">
  Quality of decisions declines with number of made choices, people get tired
  <p>`Increasing quantity of decisions`<small>might lead to impulsive and poor choices</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Anchoring effect</h3>
<Card title="Anchoring effect" icon="emoji:anchor">
  Relying too heavily on **initial information**
  <p>`Initial anchor`<small>might limit exploration of better options and lead to suboptimal decisions</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Cognitive dissonance</h3>
<Card title="Cognitive dissonance" icon="emoji:disappointed-face">
  The mental discomfort of holding **conflicting beliefs**
  <p>`Actions contradicting beliefs`<small>lead to justifying behaviors and demotivation</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Negativity bias</h3>
<Card title="Negativity bias" icon="emoji:television">
  Paying more attention to **negative information**
  <p>`Focusing on bad news`<small>can hinder motivation, creativity, and problem-solving</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Recency bias</h3>
<Card title="Recency bias" icon="emoji:telephone-receiver">
  Placing greater emphasis on **recent information**
  <p>`Highlighting the fresh memories`<small>potentially overlooking past experiences or trends</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Peak-end rule</h3>
<Card title="Peak-end rule" icon="emoji:snow-capped-mountain">
  Giving more importance to the **beginning** and **end** of an experience
  <p>`Better remembering first and last pieces`<small>can influence overall perception</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Pattern matching</h3>
<Card title="Pattern matching" icon="emoji:film-frames">
  Identifying **patterns**, even if they are not actually there
  <p>`Craving for pattern existence`<small>might lead to misinterpretations and biases</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Scarcity effect</h3>
<Card title="Scarcity effect" icon="emoji:chocolate-bar">
  The perceived value of something increases when it is seen as limited or in short supply
  <p>`Deficit`<small>increases the Fear Of Missed Opportunities</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Social desirability bias</h3>
<Card title="Social desirability bias" icon="emoji:heart-decoration">
  People desire to be seen favorably by others
  <p>`Trying to look good in others eyes`<small>can lead to false beliefs and group mistakes</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Endowment effect</h3>
<Card title="Endowment effect" icon="emoji:fishing-pole">
  Valuing own things more than things other's even if the objective value is the same
  <p>`Undervaluing others`<small>can lead misjudgement and negativity</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Ego depletion</h3>
<Card title="Ego depletion" icon="emoji:person-in-bed">
  Using the limited energy of your willpower
  <p>`Tired people are lazy`<small>can lead to whatever like decisions</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Decoy effect</h3>
<Card title="Decoy effect" icon="emoji:popcorn">
  An easy to discard extra option changes the proposition value for the others
  <p>`Unsuitable option`<small>leads to decisions based on comparative values</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Von Restorff effect</h3>
<Card title="Von Restorff effect" icon="emoji:hot-pepper">
  Users notice items that stand out among the rest more
  <p>`Distinctive item`<small>attracts more attention</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Spark effect</h3>
<Card title="Spark effect" icon="emoji:bellhop-bell">
  People are more likely to take action when the effort is small
  <p>`Small action is easier to accept`<small>tiny step boosts motivation to do it</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Curiosity gap</h3>
<Card title="Curiosity gap" icon="emoji:magnifying-glass-tilted-left">
  Evolutionary, we desire to seek out for missing information
  <p>`Brain fulfills missing parts`<small>attracts to wonder</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Skeuomorphism</h3>
<Card title="Skeuomorphism" icon="emoji:joystick">
  It's easier to adapt to more familiar things
  <p>`Things mimicking real-world ones`<small>are more familiar and require less cognitive load</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Variable reward</h3>
<Card title="Variable reward" icon="emoji:slot-machine">
  We tend to enjoy the unexpected rewards more
  <p>`Unexpected rewards`<small>are more enjoyable</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Survivorship bias</h3>
<Card title="Survivorship bias" icon="emoji:small-airplane">
  Focusing only on the “surviving” observations, excluding points that didn’t survive
  <p>`Considering only success subgroup`<small>mistakenly makes the data set incomplete</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Reactance</h3>
<Card title="Reactance" icon="emoji:volcano">
  People are less likely to adopt a behavior when they feel forced.
  <p>`Forcing behaviour may backfire`<small>be rejected & make opponent false-beliefs/beliefs stronger</small></p>
</Card>
### <h3 class="hidden">Miller’s law</h3>
<Card title="Miller’s law" icon="emoji:telephone">
  Humans can only keep ~7 items in their working memory
  <p>`Keeping in mind ~7 items`<small> – more is exhausting and usually inefficient</small></p>
</Card>
