### <h3 class="hidden">Aesthetic-Usability effect</h3>
<Card title="Aesthetic-Usability effect" icon="emoji:gem-stone">

### <h3 class="hidden">Serial Position effect</h3>
<Card title="Serial position effect" icon="emoji:chequered-flag">
  People tend to **recall items** at the **beginning** (primacy effect) & **end** (recency effect) of a list better than those in the middle
  <p>`Better remembering first and last pieces`<small>influences overall perception</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Von Restorff effect</h3>
<Card title="The Isolation effect" icon="emoji:crayon">

  <p>`Visually distinctive info`<small>attracts user attention</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Zeigarnik effect</h3>
<Card title="Zeigarnik effect" icon="emoji:doughnut">

  <p>`Sense of artificial progress`<small>increases motivation to complete task</small></p>
</Card>

## Gestalt Design principles ğŸ¨

### <h3 class="hidden">Proximity</h3>
<Card title="Proximity" icon="emoji:horizontal-traffic-light">
  Objects that are **near or proximate** to each other, tend to be **grouped together**
  <p>`Grouping related items`<small>help to organize & comprehend information faster</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Similarity</h3>
<Card title="Similarity" icon="emoji:puzzle-piece">
  Human eye tends to perceive **similar elements** in a design **as a complete picture**
  <p>`Visually similar elements perceived as related`<small>use colour, shape & size consciously</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Closure</h3>
<Card title="Closure" icon="emoji:panda">
  Mind automatically fills the gaps between elements to perceive a complete image
  <p>`We see the whole first`<small>cleverly placed elements forming the whole are appreciated</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Continuity</h3>
<Card title="Continuity" icon="emoji:sewing-needle">
  Human eye follows the paths, lines, and curves of a design & prefers to see a continuous flow of visual elements rather than separated objects
  <p>`Eye follows the direction even through obstacles`<small>align element to interlink them</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Common Region</h3>
<Card title="Common Region" icon="emoji:steaming-bowl">
  Elements **sharing an area** with a clearly defined boundary **perceived as group**
  <p>`Common region isolates the group`<small>use border and background to effectively show relationship between elements and sections</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Common Fate</h3>
<Card title="Common Fate" icon="emoji:bird">
  Objects **moving** into same direction **perceived as group**
  <p>`Common fate unites the group`<small>use movement to show relationship between elements</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Multistability</h3>
<Card title="Multistability" icon="emoji:ice-cube">
  **Ambiguous images** can be interpreted in multiple ways, but **not simultaneously**<br/>
  we experience the sensation of switching between the interpretations
  <p>`Eye cannot see the multiple versions simultaneously`<small>guide the interpretation with contrast</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Figure/Ground</h3>
<Card title="Figure/Ground" icon="emoji:chess-pawn">
  Brain perceives elements as either **objects of focus** (the figure) **or background** (ground)
  <p>`Brain separates the foreground from the background`<small>distinguish figure, create a sense of visual focus</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Uniform Connectedness</h3>
<Card title="Uniform Connectedness" icon="emoji:atom-symbol">
  Human eye tends to perceive **similar elements** in a design **as a complete picture**
  <p>`Visually connected elements perceived as related`<small>use border, lines & arrows to emphasize the items relationship</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Symmetry and Order</h3>
<Card title="Symmetry and Order" icon="emoji:abacus">
  **Symmetrically arranged** visual elements usually **perceived as grouped**
  <p>`Mind tends to favor symmetrical forms`<small>grid systems help to keep visual order</small></p>
</Card>

### <h3 class="hidden">Focal Point</h3>
<Card title="Focal Point" icon="emoji:nazar-amulet">
  **Highlighted** main element or object becomes the **center of attention**
  <p>`Emphasis captures & hold attention`<small>distinguish the most important</small></p>
</Card>
